Lamp

Please see the parts below

Casing

  • A casing with a diffusion layer that protects the lamp
  • Requires a lot of strength to take apart
  • Durable and somewhat waterproof
  • Dimensions: 18.5 cm tall and has a radius of 5 cm.
  • The type of screws should be changed as we had a hard time looking for the correct screwdriver (just change it to a phillips)

Battery

  • 4 Li-Ion batteries strapped together and soldered to both pcbs
  • Provides power for the lamp
  • Simple solution, very easy to implement
  • Adds a lot of weight, Li-Pos might be heavier but for the lamp, the li-po battery would be lighter (probably)
  • 6.5x3.3x3.6 cm

PCB (1)

  • Simple PCB with a button and a TTRS Jack for charging (check products 1 or 2 for the description of what a PCB is)
  • Copper, solder mask, and fr4 (probably)
  • Made of fiberglass reinforced epoxy resin (FR4) and copper.
  • Easy and simple to manufacture
  • susceptible to a lot

PCB (2)

  • A pcb with lights soldered on and recieves power from the battery
  • Made of aluminium and (maybe) copper
  • Easy and simple to manufacture
  • susceptible to a lot

Foam

  • Foam that encases the battery
  • Encases the battery to prevent rattling of the batteries
  • Easy way to fix down the battery
  • Adds weight to the lamp and makes disassembly harder due to friction.

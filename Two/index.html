GTX 1650 Ventus graphics card

Please see the parts below

PCB

  • Board that all components are soldered onto
  • Video connectors, chip, VRAM, voltage regulators and other components soldered on, PCIe connector to mount onto motherboard
  • Made of FR4 (fiberglass material bonded with resin) and copper for the traces.
  • Eliminates need for most wires, easy to manufacure and solder things onto
  • High heat can damage the PCB, but automated soldering with a hot air gun minimizes this risk.
  • Measures 17.1 cm x 10 cm

Fan

  • Two fans that cool down the heatsink
  • Made of mainly copper, magnets and plastic
  • Simple, cheap and easy solution to cool heatsink
  • Not the best solution, and acceleates dust accumulation
  • 7.2 cm x 7.2 x 1.1 x 0.1 cm

Heatsink

  • Large block of aluminium that conducts heat away from the chip
  • Sometimes made of copper but in this case aluminium, because of heat conductivity
  • Fins increase surface area so that the fans can cool it faster (larger surface for the air to cool)
  • Not as good as water cooling
  • Neasures 8.3 cm x 16.1 cm x 1.6 cm.
  • Note: A smaller heatsink is present (for voltage regulators, capacitors, or inductors)

Chip

  • Main computing unit for graphics
  • Mainly made of silicon, with some copper and other metals
  • Good for generating graphics, better than integrated
  • It's a bit slow compared to modern chips, creates a lot of heat
  • Measures roughly 4x4x0.2 cm

    • Unfortunately, our only image of this is with it covered in thermal paste

  • Slightly sticky pad that conducts heat
  • Conducts heat, helps heat reach to places where paste cannot reach (pads are thicker, so if one part cannot reach the heatsink with paste, use pads)

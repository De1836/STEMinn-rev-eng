PCB

  • Board that all components are soldered onto
  • Video connectors, chip, VRAM, voltage regulators and other components soldered on, PCIe connector to mount onto motherboard
  • Made of FR4 (fiberglass material bonded with resin) and copper for the traces.
  • Eliminates need for most wires, easy to manufacure and solder things onto
  • High heat can damage the PCB, but automated soldering with a hot air gun minimizes this risk.
  • Measures 17.1 cm x 10 cm

Fan

  • Two fans that cool down the heatsink
  • Made of copper and magnets, plastic
  • Simple, cheap and easy solution to cool heatsink
  • Not the best solution, and acceleates dust accumulation
  • A middle part ensures no contact between the magnet and coils, reducing wear and tear.

Heatsink

  • The heatsink is a large block made of thermally conductive material, in this case aluminum.
  • Fins on the heatsink increase surface area and the rate of heat transfer.
  • The heatsink conducts heat away from the chip and VRAM, the components generating the most heat.
  • Thermal paste connects the heatsink and chip, filling gaps and increasing heat transfer.
  • Thermal pads connect the VRAM to the heatsink instead of thermal paste.
  • The main heatsink is screwed onto the PCB and measures 8.3 cm x 16.1 cm x 1.6 cm.
  • A smaller heatsink is connected to smaller chips (e.g., voltage regulators, capacitors, or inductors) using thermal pads and expansion screws.

Cheap handheld fan

Please see the parts below

Casing

  • The thing that encases the fan and the rest of the components.
  • Made of plain white ABS plastic, shaped like an upside-down snowman.
  • Manufacturers claim it has an ergonomic grip (it sort of does).
  • Quite durable and cheap to manufacture, but is not easy to disassemble.
  • Measures 13.5 x 8.2 x 2.3cm.

PCB

  • A board with holes and pads, and connections made of copper
  • Made of Silkscreen, Soldermask, Copper and Substrate
  • Clean and tidy since traces are used instead of wires
  • Quite susceptible to external factors
  • 3.5 cm x 2 cm x 0.1 cm

Fan

  • Simple motor attached to fan blades
  • Receives power from the battery and routes it through coils
  • Wires are very easy to break, and may overheat if left running for a long time
  • 2 cm x 2 cm x 1.5 cm
  • Made of rubber and plastic for the fan, copper and magnets for the motor + another PCB which we won't count

Button

  • A button soldered onto the PCB that controls the fan
  • Allows fan to be simple to operate
  • Could be replaced with a switch instead
  • Around 0.5 cm x 0.5 cm x 0.1 cm
  • Made of hard, white ABS plastic for the cover, aluminium, and plastic for the actual button.
